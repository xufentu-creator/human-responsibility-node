## Overview

This repository presents the theoretical framework of the **Human Responsibility Node** within high-replication systems.

The work develops a structural analytical model for examining responsibility concentration, positional stability, and responsibility drift in distributed decision environments.

The framework situates responsibility as a **positional structural variable** within decision chains, defined by the intersection of:

- Decision Acceptance  
- Authority Positioning  
- Consequence Continuity  

Under high-replication conditions, execution tempo may exceed interpretive tempo. The theory analyzes how tempo asymmetry affects responsibility concentration and structural stability.

---

## Theoretical Context

This framework operates within the intersection of:

- Complex Systems Theory  
- AI Governance  
- Decision Architecture  
- Distributed Systems  

Responsibility is treated not as a normative allocation concept, but as a structural position variable that can be analyzed in terms of intersection, diffusion, and drift.
